Moomoo · Fintech KPI Design
Redefining Growth KPI for Moomoo US
Situation Growth KPIs focused on PC volume and total AUM drove teams toward short-term, low-quality acquisition — poor retention and low ROI.
Approach Diagnosed KPI-driven behavior across BD and channels. Redesigned framework around Focused, Achievable, Trackable principles — shifted primary KPI from PCs to effective traders (≥2 trades), added CAC and retention metrics weighted by channel.
↑ Impact ARPU up · CAC down · high-retention segment mix grew meaningfully · LTV for effective traders materially outpaced acquisition-only users
Moomoo · Fintech User Activation
Diagnosing the First-Trade Activation Gap
Situation A large share of paid new users never placed a first trade within the onboarding window — substantial user backlog with zero trading revenue contribution.
Approach Multi-channel uplift analysis (feed, course, in-app ads, EDM, push). Discovered legacy-user EDM was suppressing new-user conversion significantly. Built high-intent behavioral signal framework as real-time targeting triggers.
↑ Impact Significant conversion lift across all channels tested · EDM suppression effect eliminated · content sharing and watchlist signals deployed as live triggers
Moomoo · Fintech Lifecycle Analytics
Crypto User Lifecycle Segmentation
Situation A small segment of crypto users drove the majority of revenue — but most were dormant. Blanket promotional campaigns failed to account for where users were in their trading journey.
Approach Built lifecycle segmentation (short-term traders, wave traders, dormant high-value) based on behavioral signals. PSM + Fisher Exact Test to measure promotional uplift by segment. Surfaced BTC pullback timing as a key re-engagement signal for dormant traders.
↑ Impact Meaningful uplift for high-intent segments · behavioral timing signals operationalized into targeting · dormant reactivation strategy adopted by growth team
Moomoo · Fintech Channel Attribution
KOL Attribution & Budget Reallocation
Situation KOL marketing budget was allocated primarily by follower count — a vanity metric with weak correlation to actual conversion quality or LTV.
Approach Used DiD to isolate KOL-driven attribution and separate it from organic lift. Found significant conversion variance across creator types. Scored hundreds of partners on a combined LTV + payback model to rank true ROI.
↑ Impact Budget reallocated toward higher-converting crypto-content creators · follower count replaced by LTV-weighted scoring as primary selection criterion
Walmart · Big Retail Retention Analytics Predictive Modeling
Membership Renewal Prediction Model
Situation Rapid new-store expansion made first-year members a large share of the base — historically low renewal rates threatened a wave of non-renewals. The business had no early-warning system to identify at-risk members before the renewal window closed.
Approach Built a behavioral scoring model predicting Year-1 renewal risk from engagement signals (visit frequency, category breadth, online activity, NPS complaints). Validated via backtesting. Deployed as an automated batch pipeline that periodically re-scores the full member base and surfaces intervention-ready cohorts to CRM.
↑ Impact Measurable renewal rate improvement · model operationalized as live scoring pipeline · enabled proactive outreach to at-risk members before renewal window — rather than reacting after lapse
Walmart · Big Retail KPI Design Membership Analytics
From HML Definition to Operating Model
Situation Sam's Club lacked a shared definition of high-margin member (HML) — different teams optimized for different proxies (spend, visits, renewal) with no alignment. Even where HMLs were identified, there was no systematic playbook for how to grow or protect them.
Approach Defined HML using a composite of spend depth, category breadth, and renewal trajectory. Then shifted focus to operations: mapped the behavioral journey of HMLs — what triggers them to deepen engagement, what signals precede disengagement. Translated this into channel-specific intervention playbooks for CRM, in-store ops, and marketing, with stage-appropriate KPIs for each team to own.
↑ Impact HML definition standardized across product, ops, and marketing · behavioral operating playbook embedded into CRM and in-store workflows · teams shifted from reactive retention to proactive HML cultivation
